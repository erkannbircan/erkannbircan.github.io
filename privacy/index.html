Privacy Policy

Last updated: 18 July 2026

GridPilot is a local-first macOS utility. We do not run GridPilot servers, we do not have accounts, and we never transmit your workspace data to the developer or any third party. GridPilot's workspace data stays in local files on your Mac.

What GridPilot reads

To move and arrange your windows, GridPilot uses macOS Accessibility and, in the App Store sandbox, the separate user-consented Window Automation service (System Events). These services are used only for window management. GridPilot reads:

  • Window position and size — to place windows into zones.
  • App identity (bundle identifier and app name) — to know which app a window belongs to and, for Profiles, which apps to launch.
  • Window titles — read locally only while saving a Profile or Desktop Snapshot, or later applying/restoring it, only when the saved group contains multiple windows from the same app and a title is needed to distinguish sibling windows. Titles are never read for single-window groups.
  • URLs you add to a Profile yourself — stored so a Profile can reopen them.

GridPilot never reads the contents of your windows, your screen, your keystrokes, your clipboard, your files, or your browsing history.

Where the data lives

Workspace data is stored in GridPilot's macOS Application Support directory:

  • App Store build: ~/Library/Containers/app.gridpilot.mac/Data/Library/Application Support/GridPilot/
  • Development or other non-sandboxed build: ~/Library/Application Support/GridPilot/

Profiles and snapshots can contain app identities, window geometry, the limited window titles described above, and Profile URLs. Settings, custom layouts, and local license or entitlement state are stored separately in the same GridPilot directory. None of these files is uploaded, synced, or shared by GridPilot.

macOS does not guarantee that moving an app to the Trash will remove its Application Support files. Use the in-app deletion control before uninstalling if you want its saved Profiles and Desktop Snapshots erased.

Telemetry

GridPilot contains no analytics, telemetry, advertising, tracking SDKs, or tracking domains. It does not send window information, usage data, or device identifiers to the developer or any third party.

Purchases

Apple handles purchases, subscription billing, and entitlement verification through the App Store and StoreKit. GridPilot's initial seven-day Pro trial is recorded only on your Mac; it does not start a subscription or charge you. Eligible customers may also receive a separate seven-day App Store introductory offer with the yearly subscription. GridPilot receives only the verified entitlement status Apple provides and never sees your payment details.

Your controls

  • Delete All Saved Workspace Data (menu bar → Profiles) immediately and permanently erases saved Profiles and Desktop Snapshots — including the app identities, window geometry, limited titles, and Profile URLs stored in them. It intentionally keeps your custom layouts, preferences, and local license or entitlement state.
  • Revoking Accessibility access in System Settings stops all window reading at once.
  • Revoking GridPilot's System Events access under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Automation stops the App Store build from controlling other apps' windows.
