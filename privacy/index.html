Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026

GridPilot is a local-first macOS utility. We do not run servers, we do not have accounts, and we never transmit your data anywhere. Everything GridPilot knows stays in local files on your Mac.

What GridPilot reads

To move and arrange your windows, GridPilot uses macOS Accessibility and, in the App Store sandbox, the separate user-consented Window Automation service (System Events). These services are used only for window management. GridPilot reads:

  • Window position and size — to place windows into zones.
  • App identity (bundle identifier and app name) — to know which app a window belongs to and, for Profiles, which apps to launch.
  • Window titles — read only when needed to tell apart multiple windows of the same app (for example, two Chrome windows) so that Layout Rescue and Profiles restore the right one.
  • URLs you add to a Profile yourself — stored so a Profile can reopen them.

GridPilot never reads the contents of your windows, your screen, your keystrokes, your clipboard, your files, or your browsing history.

Where the data lives

All of the above is stored locally under ~/Library/Application Support/GridPilot/ (profiles, snapshots, settings). It is never uploaded, synced, or shared. Deleting the app, or using Delete All Saved Workspace Data in the menu, removes it.

Telemetry

GridPilot contains no analytics, telemetry, advertising, tracking SDKs, or tracking domains. It does not send window information, usage data, or device identifiers to the developer or any third party.

Purchases

Apple handles purchases, subscription billing, and entitlement verification through the App Store and StoreKit. GridPilot's initial seven-day Pro trial is recorded only on your Mac; it does not start a subscription or charge you. Eligible customers may also receive a separate seven-day App Store introductory offer with the yearly subscription. GridPilot receives only the verified entitlement status Apple provides and never sees your payment details.

Your controls

  • Delete All Saved Workspace Data (menu bar → Profiles) erases all saved profiles and snapshots — including the titles and URLs above — immediately and permanently.
  • Revoking Accessibility access in System Settings stops all window reading at once.
  • Revoking GridPilot's System Events access under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Automation stops the App Store build from controlling other apps' windows.
