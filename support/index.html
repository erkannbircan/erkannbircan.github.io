Frequently asked

GridPilot isn't moving my windows

GridPilot needs Accessibility permission. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and turn on the switch next to GridPilot. If you granted it but nothing happens, quit and reopen GridPilot once so macOS re-reads the permission.

What are the keyboard shortcuts?

⌃⌘1–0 snaps the focused window into a zone. ⌃⌘←↑↓→ moves it between zones. ⌃⌘Z undoes the last move. Profiles can be assigned their own ⌃⌥⌘ + digit shortcut in the Profile editor.

How does the free trial and pricing work?

GridPilot is free for 7 days with every feature unlocked. After that you can continue with a subscription (monthly or yearly) or a one-time Lifetime unlock. Purchases and the trial are handled by Apple; you can manage or cancel any time in App Store → your account → Subscriptions.

How do I delete my saved data?

Click the GridPilot menu-bar icon → Profiles → Delete All Saved Workspace Data. This permanently removes every saved profile and layout snapshot. GridPilot stores everything locally under ~/Library/Application Support/GridPilot/ and never uploads anything.

Does GridPilot read my screen or send data anywhere?

No. GridPilot has no servers, no accounts, and no telemetry. It reads only window position, size, app identity, and — only to tell apart two windows of the same app — their titles. It never reads window contents or keystrokes. See our Privacy Policy.

My external monitor layout got scrambled after unplugging

Use Layout Rescue: GridPilot remembers each monitor arrangement and can put every window back. Open the menu-bar icon → Restore Desktop Snapshot. Redocking a known monitor also triggers an automatic restore.
